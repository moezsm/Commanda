# Commanda Pricing Model

## 1. Pricing Objective

Commanda should use a freemium pricing model that is attractive to small Tunisian social-media sellers while remaining operationally sustainable as usage grows. The free plan must support real daily work, and the premium plan must monetize productivity, collaboration, and growth, not basic order survival.

## 2. Pricing Principles

- The free plan must allow a solo seller to run the business daily.
- Limits should control storage and operational cost without making the product frustrating.
- Premium should unlock time-saving and scale-oriented value.
- Pricing must be simple to understand on mobile.
- Tunisia launch pricing should be accessible, with room for country-specific pricing later.
- Large sellers should pay when their usage creates higher operational load or when they need advanced workflows.

## 3. Recommended Packaging Structure

| Plan | Positioning | Main user | Revenue role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | Essential daily order management | Solo seller starting out | Acquisition and retention |
| Premium | Growth and productivity tools | Active seller with recurring order volume | Core subscription revenue |
| Later add-ons | Compliance or advanced operations | Mature or multi-market seller | Expansion revenue |

## 4. Free Plan Recommendation

### Goal

The free plan should be useful enough that sellers can adopt it as their daily order notebook replacement.

### Included capabilities

- Create and update orders.
- Save customer records.
- Save and reuse delivery companies.
- Assign a delivery company per order.
- Track core order statuses.
- Search recent orders and customers.
- View basic order history for each customer.
- Archive completed orders.
- Access the app on mobile without feature crippling.

### Suggested free-plan limits

These limits are designed to protect storage and performance while preserving usability.

| Limit area | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Active orders | Generous but capped, for example 100 to 200 active orders | Keeps free plan operational for small sellers |
| Archived orders | Allowed, but with archive-first retrieval rules | Preserves user trust while protecting performance |
| Team members | 1 user only | Team workflows are a strong premium lever |
| Product catalog | Manual order product entry only | Keeps MVP simple and protects premium upsell |
| Exports | Not included or highly limited | Good premium differentiator |
| Advanced analytics | Not included | Strong premium value |
| Integrations | Not included | Keeps launch cost low |

### Best free-limit direction

The strongest launch position is not to heavily restrict order creation, but to restrict advanced workflow features. If a hard cap is used, it should be on active operational volume rather than total historical data. Historical data can remain accessible through archive views.

## 5. Premium Plan Recommendation

### Goal

Premium should appeal to sellers who are growing, handling more orders, or beginning to work with assistants.

### Recommended premium inclusions

- Higher or unlimited active order capacity.
- Team access and role-based permissions.
- Bulk updates and batch actions.
- Advanced filters and reporting.
- Reusable product catalog.
- Customer insights and repeat-buyer metrics.
- Courier integration roadmap access.
- Export and download tools.
- Branded documents and labels later.

### Premium positioning message

Free helps a seller stay organized. Premium helps a seller save time, manage volume, and run operations professionally.

## 6. Later Add-On Revenue Opportunities

These should not be launched immediately.

- E-invoicing module.
- France-specific compliance features.
- Multi-store or multi-brand management.
- API or ERP-style integrations.
- Messaging credits or notification bundles.

## 7. Recommended Monetization Path by Stage

### Stage 1: Adoption

- Launch free plan first or with a soft premium waitlist.
- Validate that sellers use Commanda every day after receiving orders.
- Track when users hit natural friction from growth rather than arbitrary restrictions.

### Stage 2: First conversions

- Introduce one premium tier.
- Focus on team access, analytics, bulk actions, and advanced filtering.
- Avoid multiple confusing tiers too early.

### Stage 3: Market expansion

- Localize pricing by market.
- Add country-specific premium modules.
- Keep compliance tools as separate value layers rather than bloating the base plan.

## 8. Tunisia-First Pricing Strategy

The exact price should be validated with local market interviews, but the structure should be:

- Free: strong enough to replace notebooks or spreadsheets for a solo seller.
- Premium monthly subscription: affordable for a seller with stable order volume.
- Optional annual pricing later for cash-flow stability.

### Pricing logic

- Price should feel cheaper than the time lost from manual order confusion.
- It should be realistic for a micro-business, not benchmarked against enterprise SaaS.
- Premium value should be obvious in saved time and reduced mistakes.

## 9. France and Africa Expansion Pricing Considerations

### France

- Higher willingness to pay is possible.
- Stronger demand for reporting, invoicing, and compliance.
- Premium packaging can be broader and more structured.

### Selected African markets

- Affordability remains essential.
- Operational needs may stay close to Tunisia's social-commerce workflow.
- Local courier and language differences matter more than advanced feature depth at first.

## 10. Usage Rules That Protect Performance

Pricing and architecture should work together.

- Use per-workspace limits, not global usage assumptions.
- Default views should only load active orders.
- Archived data should be searchable separately.
- Premium bulk jobs should run asynchronously when needed.
- Larger sellers should trigger premium conversion before their operational pattern becomes costly on the free tier.

## 11. What Should Not Be Paywalled

To keep the free plan credible, these should remain available in some form:

- Creating an order.
- Saving a customer.
- Assigning a delivery company.
- Updating order status.
- Accessing archived history.

If these are restricted too early, adoption will drop because the product stops being a true operational tool.

## 12. Recommended Final Pricing Summary

Commanda should launch with one strong free plan and one clear premium plan. The free plan should cover the complete core workflow for a solo seller: order capture, client management, delivery company assignment, status tracking, search, and archive access. Premium should unlock the features that matter when a seller grows: team usage, analytics, bulk actions, reporting, product catalog tools, and integrations.

This structure matches the Tunisia-first market, protects the product's growth engine, and supports later expansion into France and African markets without redesigning the business model.
